Work

I am a devoted father of four lovely daughters and a tech enthusiast with a diverse background in entrepreneurship and a passion for learning. My professional journey started over two decades ago in the retail industry, where I gained expertise in human resource and data-driven management, account and assortment management, stakeholder collaboration, and customer service - not forgetting marketing. I'm also an avid learner who is studying to master the art of coding. I'm trying to find the ways to develop myself more and -why not- completely new profession.

My professional journey commenced when I was a seventeen-year-old college student and was discovered right outside the doorstep of my family's apartment. From the bottle and cart boy in the beginning to present work as a K-retailer entrepreneur I have done all the different jobs in a grocery store one possibly can.

Throughout my career I have had the privilege of working with numerous superiors and collaborators and with two different K-retailers. These opportunities have enabled me to develop the expertise to work effectively with various individuals and teams. As an entrepreneur for over a decade, I have gained invaluable experience in leading both employees and teams. Some of my key entrepreneurial qualities include the ability to withstand stress, make swift decisions and work with a solution-based approach.

When I started in the retail industry in February 1997, ordering and assortment management were mostly manual - orders were made by calling and the quantity was always decided by the purchaser. However, at that time, there were already portable devices available that transmitted orders electronically to suppliers.

The operations of the retail industry inevitably began to utilize more information technology, and soon we were able to enjoy the first time when orders were made by the machine. However this had nothing to do with artificial intelligence - the algorithms were simple at that time.

Currently, my career as a K-retailer has reached a point where, due to the termination of the lease for the second store's premises at the end of this year, it is difficult to sustain my livelihood meaningfully with just one store. Therefore, I've started to look for new job opportunities. It would be convenient and pleasant to be able to combine the experience gained as an entrepreneur with studies/hobbies, such as coding.

Studies

It has always been easy for me to adopt information technology as part of my daily work. I have been interested in what can be found under the hood of an application or a system - why something works the way it does. Nowadays, most of our tools are cloud based and are used on mobile devices or computers.

I have always loved different kind of systems, computers and gadgets. The desire to find out what is the logic behind them and the fast growing digitalization in the field of grocery trade - especially inside K group - drove me to apply to LAB University of Applied Sciences. After passing the entrance exams I found myself studying to become an engineer of information and communication technology. All the studies are remote studies and I have studied little less than two years. It is of course sometimes a bit hard to combine work, studies and family life. With good time management, even that can be accomplished.

The war in Ukraine impacted business operations in a way that required me to be more involved in my company's work. The second year of studies was also more intense than the first and it began to be difficult to study efficiently while working. I had to put my studies on hold, but I have continued to study independently with the courses I obtained from the internet.

As part of my studies, I did an internship in the Kesko artificial intelligence unit and after studying the Python programming language used by the unit day and night, I eventually managed to code somewhat independently. Today, a small part of the code in the assortment management application used by every K-food store was written by me.
I have returned to school and new semester will start at the beginning of August 2024.

The release of ChatGPT for public use in late 2022 made the world realize just how advanced AI models have become. My interest in AI led me to learn more about the topic beyond just reading news headlines, and our first encounter extended into the late hours of the night. Even my studies and fascination with systems and computers did not prepare me for what I discovered. In that moment I realized that this was something that would change the world significantly. From that moment on I have been slowly studying and testing what ChatGPT is capable of. The next step through ChatGPT of course was to start learning about how GitHub Copilot can help me in my coding. It was clear that in order to use artificial intelligence you must have a foundational understanding of the subject matter to which you are applying AI. Otherwise you cannot critically examine the solution options provided by the AI.
